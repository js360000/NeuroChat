NeuroChat brandkit

Calm interface. Premium dark palette. Low-friction communication.

This external brand reference stays aligned to the actual visual system in neurochat-web/src/index.css and the product surfaces used across landing, discover, messaging, and energy tracking. The goal is to let growth, partnerships, and content teams build material that looks like NeuroChat rather than generic mental-health tech.

Brand postureSupportive but not childish. Futuristic but not cold. Accessible without losing polish.
Primary usageDark-first layouts, glass surfaces, soft glows, rounded geometry, restrained contrast spikes.
TypographyLexend throughout. No secondary display family in the current frontend.

Typography

Lexend system
Lower stimulation, clearer intent, easier reading.
NeuroChat uses Lexend across headlines, support copy, and interface labels. The frontend relies on spacing, weight, and card depth rather than introducing a second expressive type family.
Headline ruleUse tight tracking and short line lengths. Major messages should feel decisive, not noisy.
Body ruleKeep line height generous and default copy in muted foreground tones to avoid glare.

Brand principles

From live product UI
Calm contrastContrast is strong enough for readability but rarely pure black-on-white or neon-on-black.
Soft futurismUse gradients, glass, and subtle glow to imply intelligence without going full cyberpunk.
Emotional precisionTone tags, status states, and safety cues should read as useful controls, not decorative stickers.
Rounded trustInterface corners are consistently soft and generous, which helps the product feel less punitive and more adaptive.
